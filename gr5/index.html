GR5 Low-Fi Prototype + Testing

Paper-first recovery flows for retail fulfillment under timer pressure.

This low-fi deliverable follows the GR5 assignment structure: solution realizations, top concepts, paper prototype task flows, testing plan, and no fabricated results.

Final direction

Phone app as the primary interface, with a watch companion for glanceable interruption support.

Three realizations selected to sketch and compare.

Watch companion

C

Cart display

Top 2

Final realizations selected to flesh out.

Phone app

Richer information density, easier onboarding, and strongest fit for multi-step recovery decisions.

Watch companion

Fast glanceability for interruptions and escalation prompts, but limited as a standalone decision tool.

Pros / Cons

Why the phone-first direction won.

Pros

  • Stronger support for complex missing-item flows
  • Better fit for substitute comparison and escalation context
  • Easier to prototype and test in paper form

Cons

  • Potentially slower to access during active picking
  • Higher visual demand during interruptions
  • Can feel like extra work if too many steps are exposed

Construction

How the paper prototype would be built and run.

  • Printer paper or cardstock for core device screens
  • Sticky overlays for modal states and confirmations
  • Index cards for alternate branches and supervisor responses
  • Phone frame and watch frame templates for realism
  • Team roles: facilitator, computer, observer / note-taker

Testing Plan

Usability goals and measurements from the GR5 brief.

Goals

  • Recover from interruptions without asking what to do next
  • Complete help-seeking and missing-item flows with low hesitation

Measures

  • Prompts or questions asked during each task
  • Time to complete each flow
  • Wrong turns or backtracks
  • Self-reported confidence

Testing Script

Three paper-prototype tasks to run with users.

Task 1

You are picking an order and the item is not on the shelf. Show me what you would do.

Task 2

You are interrupted by a customer while in the middle of an order. Help the customer, then continue your task.

Task 3

Your order is behind pace, multiple items are unavailable, and you need help. Show me how you would finish.
