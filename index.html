Just-in-time support for retail fulfillment workers under timer pressure.

Backline helps newer retail fulfillment workers recover from missing items, customer interruptions, and uncertainty-heavy decisions without losing pace or confidence.

View Deliverables See Research

Core Tensions

  • Speed vs accuracy
  • Training vs real shift judgment
  • Help-seeking vs looking competent

Research Signal

“People might think it’s just grabbing groceries off shelves. But you’re constantly deciding substitutions, figuring out where things actually are, and balancing speed with getting the order right.”

Jordan, retail fulfillment associate

Problem

Retail fulfillment looks simple from the outside, but the real work is recovery.

What workers face

Retail fulfillment associates are expected to move quickly, stay accurate, and remain calm in public-facing environments. The hardest moments are not routine picks, but breakdown moments: a missing item, a risky substitution, a customer interruption, or uncertainty about whether to ask for help.

Why existing training falls short

Our research showed that formal training covers scanner mechanics and workflow basics, but does not prepare workers for live judgment under pressure. Experience builds speed because it builds unofficial knowledge, not because workers simply move faster.

Interview highlights

  • Training taught mechanics, not judgment.
  • Help-seeking is both socially and operationally costly.
  • Interruptions break mental flow and increase recovery time.
  • Experienced workers rely on unofficial store knowledge.

Selected POV

A newer retail fulfillment associate who wants to keep pace and look competent needs fast support for ambiguous edge-case decisions because training covers the official process, but real performance depends on judgment, timing, and knowing when to escalate.

Solution Direction

Backline supports workers in the exact moments where they are most likely to hesitate.

01

Edge-case decision support

A phone-first support flow for missing items and substitutions that helps workers make fast, higher-confidence decisions without guessing.

02

Low-friction escalation

A contextual help flow that makes asking for support feel efficient instead of competence-threatening.

03

Interruption recovery

A resume flow that preserves task context when customers interrupt an active order.
